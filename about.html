About RIES

An independent society, built around a method.

RIES was formed to give serious research a home outside university funding cycles and startup timelines — where a question can take as long as it needs.

Most independent researchers face the same obstacle: institutions willing to fund a two-year study rarely tolerate a finding that takes three. RIES was founded in 2023 as the research division of AMAZE Consortium to remove that constraint — providing structure, peer review, and modest funding to researchers working outside traditional academic or corporate settings, while asking nothing of them but rigor.

In practice: every study accepted into RIES is assigned a review pair — one member inside the researcher's field, one deliberately outside it — before work begins, not after.

How Work Moves Through RIES

Four stages, no exceptions.

01

Inquiry

A proposal is examined for a testable question, not a foregone conclusion.

02

Evidence

Data collection is logged openly; raw findings are recorded before interpretation begins.

03

Review

Two assigned reviewers — one specialist, one generalist — challenge the method before the conclusion.

04

Publication

Work is released with its data, its dissent, and its limitations intact.

An external advisory board of eleven researchers, unaffiliated with RIES day-to-day operations, reviews contested findings on request. Their dissent is published alongside the work, not filed separately.

RIES operates as the research division of AMAZE Consortium, which provides administrative and funding infrastructure. Editorial and research decisions are made independently by RIES leadership and its review board.
