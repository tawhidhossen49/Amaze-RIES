What eleven months in a mangrove forest teaches you about patience

Field researcher Rana Chowdhury on the difference between a dataset that looks finished and one that actually is.

By Rana Chowdhury · July 2026 · 6 min read

The instinct, three months into any field study, is to see a pattern early and start writing toward it. The Sundarbans project taught us to distrust that instinct specifically around month four — which is exactly when the data starts to look convincing and is exactly when it's usually still wrong.

Continue reading →

By month eleven, two of the four monitoring stations had been rebuilt after storm damage, and the dataset that finally held up looked nothing like the one the team was ready to publish in month four. The lesson wasn't to work faster — it was to build review checkpoints early enough to catch a convincing story before it hardens into a conclusion.
