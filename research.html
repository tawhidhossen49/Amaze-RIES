Method

Four stages, no exceptions.

01

Inquiry

Proposals must state a falsifiable question. We reject roughly 40% of submissions at this stage — most commonly because the question already assumes its answer.

02

Evidence

Data is logged in a shared, timestamped record as it's collected — not reconstructed afterward from notes.

03

Review

A specialist reviewer checks the method; a generalist reviewer checks whether the conclusion actually follows from the data.

04

Publication

Papers are released with raw data, review notes, and any unresolved disagreement between reviewers.

Featured — March 2026

Mapping tidal erosion along the Sundarbans coastline

R. Chowdhury, Lead Field Researcher, RIES Coastal Systems Group

Over twenty-two months, four monitoring stations tracked sediment displacement across a twelve-kilometer stretch of mangrove coastline. Erosion accelerated non-linearly in zones adjacent to root systems damaged by upstream dredging, suggesting root density functions as a threshold variable rather than a linear buffer.

Additional Authors: M. Islam · A. Osei · S. Rahman · T. Banerjee · K. Huda

Field access supported by three coastal fishing cooperatives; equipment funded by AMAZE Consortium's Field Research Grant.

Every published study is accompanied by its underlying dataset, released under an open license unless a participant's privacy requires otherwise. We would rather a finding be wrong in public than unverifiable in private.
